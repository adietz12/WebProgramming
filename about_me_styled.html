About me

Henry Kissinger

I am a German-born U.S. political diplomat and foreign-policy adviser. I emigrated to the U.S. in 1938 and taught at Harvard for a little while. President Nixon then appointed me to assistant for national security affairs. Then from 1969 to 1975 I served as head of the national security council in the UN. I was eventually promoted to secretary of state from 1973 to 1977.


My biggest accomplishments come from my work as a diplomat and advisor to president Nixon. I developed a policy of detente with the Soviet Union which lead to the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks agreement. I also initiated the first U.S. contact with China and engaged in shuttle democracy with the Middle East. I was also a huge advocate for the Vietnam war, so much so that I was able to initiate a massive bombing campaign in Cambodia during it. Some people might call me a "war criminal" for it, but it helped the U.S. reach a stalemate with Vietnam.
